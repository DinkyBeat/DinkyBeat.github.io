Problems I am interested in (2026)

Pr1 (DeterministicFactorization). Let $\mathbb{F}_q$ be a finite field and $f(x)\in\mathbb{F}_q[x]$ a nonconstant polynomial. Randomized algorithms of Berlekamp and Cantor--Zassenhaus factor $f(x)$ in time polynomial in $\deg f$ and $\log q$. It is unknown whether there exists a deterministic algorithm that factors arbitrary polynomials in $\mathbb{F}_q[x]$ in polynomial time without relying on unproven hypotheses, and even restricted structural families remain poorly understood.
Pr2 (StanleyDepth). For a monomial ideal $I\subset S=k[x_1,\dots,x_n]$, one defines the combinatorial invariant $\operatorname{sdepth}(I)$ (Stanley depth) via Stanley decompositions, while $\operatorname{depth}(I)$ is the homological depth. Although these invariants arise from very different constructions, no general inequality relating them is known in full generality, and whether \[ \operatorname{sdepth}(I)\ge \operatorname{depth}(I) \] holds for all monomial ideals remains unresolved.
Pr3 (ReflexiveUnimodality). Let $P\subset\mathbb{R}^d$ be a reflexive lattice polytope and write its Ehrhart series as \[ \sum_{m\ge0} |mP\cap\mathbb{Z}^d|\,t^m=\frac{h^*(t)}{(1-t)^{d+1}}, \] where $h^*(t)=h_0^*+h_1^*t+\cdots+h_d^*t^d$. The coefficients form a symmetric vector, but it is unknown whether the sequence $(h_i^*)$ must always be unimodal.
Pr4 (IDPUnimodality). A lattice polytope $P$ has the integer decomposition property (IDP) if every lattice point of $mP$ decomposes as a sum of $m$ lattice points of $P$. The associated semigroup algebra is normal, yet it is unknown whether IDP implies unimodality of the Ehrhart $h^*$-vector or other strong positivity properties.
Pr5 (WilfSemigroup). Let $S=\langle a_1,\dots,a_e\rangle\subset\mathbb{N}$ be a numerical semigroup with Frobenius number $F$ and genus $g=|\mathbb{N}\setminus S|$. Wilf's conjecture asserts that \[ F+1\le e\,g. \] Despite verification in many cases, no general proof or counterexample is known.
Pr6 (CasasAlvero). Let $f(x)\in k[x]$ be a polynomial of degree $n$ over a field of characteristic $0$. If $\gcd(f,f^{(i)})\neq1$ for every derivative $1\le i\le n-1$, must $f(x)$ be a pure power $(x-a)^n$? This deceptively elementary condition defines a highly constrained algebraic locus whose global structure is not understood.
Pr7 (ZariskiCancellation). Let $k$ be a field and $A$ a finitely generated $k$-algebra. If \[ A[t]\cong k[x_1,\dots,x_{n+1}] \] as $k$-algebras, must $A\cong k[x_1,\dots,x_n]$? This is the Zariski Cancellation Problem, solved in low dimensions but open or subtle in many geometric settings.
Pr8 (RationalVsUnirational). A projective variety $X$ over $k$ is unirational if there exists a dominant rational map $\mathbb{P}^n\dashrightarrow X$, and rational if it is birational to $\mathbb{P}^n$. Although rational $\Rightarrow$ unirational, the converse is poorly understood in higher dimensions, and explicit separating examples remain difficult to construct.
Pr9 (NagataInterpolation). Given general points $p_1,\dots,p_r\in\mathbb{P}^2$ with multiplicities $m_i$, determine the minimal degree $d$ for which there exists a plane curve of degree $d$ vanishing to order $m_i$ at each $p_i$. Nagata predicted precise asymptotic bounds, but the conjecture is open except in special cases.
Pr10 (GroupRingTorsion). Let $G$ be a finite group and consider the unit group of the integral group ring $\mathbb{Z}[G]$. The Zassenhaus conjecture predicts that every torsion unit is conjugate in $\mathbb{Q}[G]$ to an element of $G$, yet this remains unresolved in general.
Pr11 (GenericInitialIdeals). For a homogeneous ideal $I\subset k[x_1,\dots,x_n]$, the generic initial ideal $\mathrm{gin}(I)$ encodes asymptotic behavior under coordinate changes. Even for ideals generated by two general forms, there is no closed description of $\mathrm{gin}(I)$ or its Betti numbers.
Pr12 (CellularResolutions). Many monomial ideals admit minimal free resolutions supported on CW-complexes. It is unknown which classes of ideals always admit such cellular resolutions, and no intrinsic characterization of this property is known.
Pr13 (gConjectureSpheres). The $g$-conjecture predicts necessary and sufficient conditions for a sequence to occur as the $h$-vector of a simplicial sphere. While proven for polytopes, the general topological case remains open.
Pr14 (PermutationBinomials). A polynomial $f(x)\in\mathbb{F}_q[x]$ is a permutation polynomial if it induces a bijection of $\mathbb{F}_q$. Determining when sparse polynomials such as $x^a+cx^b$ have this property is known only in scattered families.
Pr15 (SparsePermutationPolynomials). For trinomials and quadrinomials over finite fields, no classification exists describing when they define permutations. Their behavior appears governed by subtle arithmetic relations among exponents.
Pr16 (AlmostCompleteIntersectionHilbert). If $I=(f_1,\dots,f_{c+1})$ in a polynomial ring has codimension $c$, then $I$ is an almost complete intersection. The possible Hilbert functions and graded Betti tables of such ideals lack a structural classification.
Pr17 (SymbolicEquality). For an ideal $I\subset R$, the symbolic power \[ I^{(m)}=\bigcap_{\mathfrak{p}\in\mathrm{Min}(I)}(I^mR_{\mathfrak{p}}\cap R) \] often differs from the ordinary power $I^m$. Determining when equality holds is a central unresolved question in the theory of singularities and blowups.
Pr18 (SymbolicContainment). Given integers $m,r$, determining when the containment \[ I^{(m)}\subseteq I^r \] holds is known only through partial bounds (e.g.\ Ein--Lazarsfeld--Smith, Hochster--Huneke). Sharp criteria depending on the geometry of $\mathrm{Spec}(R/I)$ are unknown.
Pr19 (RegularityAsymptotics). For a homogeneous ideal $I$, the Castelnuovo--Mumford regularity satisfies \[ \mathrm{reg}(I^m)=am+b \] for $m\gg0$. While asymptotic linearity is known, there is no general method to determine $a$ or $b$ from the generators of $I$.
Pr20 (ReesEquations). The Rees algebra \[ \mathcal{R}(I)=\bigoplus_{m\ge0} I^m t^m \] controls the blowup of $\mathrm{Spec}(R)$ along $V(I)$. Even for simple ideals, describing defining equations, depth, or Cohen--Macaulayness of $\mathcal{R}(I)$ is widely open.
